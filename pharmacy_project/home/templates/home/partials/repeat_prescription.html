What is a repeat prescription?

A repeat prescription allows you to easily reorder your regular medications without needing to see your GP each time. This service is designed to make managing ongoing treatments more convenient and efficient.

Our team works closely with your GP practice to ensure that your prescriptions are processed accurately and ready for collection at your preferred branch. We aim to provide a quick, reliable, and stress-free experience.

How does our repeat prescription service work?

How to order

Patients can order repeat prescriptions through their GP surgery using their NHS account, online services, or by contacting the surgery.

Repeat dispensing

If a GP has set up repeat dispensing, a patient may be able to ask their pharmacy directly for their medication.

Electronic repeat prescriptions

eRD allows a prescriber to authorize a batch of repeatable prescriptions for up to 12 months. The prescriptions are automatically downloaded to the patient’s pharmacy at set intervals.

Emergency repeat medications

In clinically exceptional circumstances, NHS 111 may supply emergency repeat medications outside of hours.

Checking prescriptions

When collecting or having prescriptions delivered, patients should check the bag before leaving the pharmacy. If there are any medications that the patient doesn't need, they can return them to the pharmacy.

How to order a repeat prescription

If you take medicine regularly, you'll usually have a repeat prescription. This means you can order your medicine when you need it, without having to see a GP every time.

You might be able to ask your pharmacy directly for your medicine if your GP has set this up (repeat dispensing). If not, you'll need to order your medicines through a repeat prescription from your GP surgery.

There are 3 ways to order a repeat prescription from your GP surgery:

  • Using your NHS account.
  • Using other online services or apps.
  • Contacting your GP surgery.
