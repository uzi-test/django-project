How it works

A pharmacist uses a microscope and a low-pressure suction device to gently remove earwax from your ear canal.

Benefits

Microsuction is a quick procedure that doesn’t use water, making it cleaner and more comfortable than other methods. Most patients notice an immediate improvement in hearing and symptoms after treatment.

When it’s used

Microsuction is recommended for people who shouldn’t have water in their ears, such as those with a perforated eardrum, a history of thick ear discharge, or patients who have had ear surgery.

Aftercare

After the procedure, you should avoid strenuous activity for 1–2 days. You can take over-the-counter pain relievers if you experience any discomfort.

Side effects

Possible side effects include dizziness, mild discomfort, reduced hearing, infection, or trauma to the ear. Our pharmacists will guide you and ensure the procedure is as safe as possible.

Who needs earwax microsuction?

Microsuction is used for people who should not have water in their ears, especially if they have:

  • Any serious complications from a previous irrigation with water
  • Had a middle ear infection (otitis media) in the last 2 months
  • Had any form of ear surgery apart from grommets in the last 2 years
  • A hole (perforation) in their ear drum or a history of thick discharge
  • A cleft palate (repaired or not)
  • A painful ear infection (acute otitis externa)
