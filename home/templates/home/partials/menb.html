Meningitis B (MenB)

Meningitis B (MenB) is a serious bacterial infection that can cause meningitis and septicaemia. It's most common in babies and young children, but can affect people of any age

Symptoms

Symptoms fever, poor feeding, vomiting, lethargy, and purple rash.

Risk

Meningitis B can lead to long-term health problems such as: amputation, deafness, epilepsy, and difficulty with learning.

Prevention

The MenB vaccine is routinely offered to babies at 8, 16 weeks, and 12 to 13 months. The vaccine helps protect babies against MenB.
