What is it?

The Qdenga vaccine is available in the UK for people aged four and older who have had dengue in the past. It is not suitable for people who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a weakened immune system.

Dengue, also known as dengue fever, is an infection spread by mosquitoes. It’s not usually serious and often gets better on its own. Some people get a more severe type of dengue, but this is rare.

Risk areas

Dengue fever is a risk in tropical and sub-tropical regions, including parts of Africa, the Americas, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. It’s also a risk in some parts of Europe, such as southern France and northern Italy.

Length of protection

Up to 4½ years.

Travellers should have completed or be up-to-date with a primary vaccination course according to the NHS vaccination schedule.
