Flu is predicted to be severe this year. It's important to get your flu vaccination and help you and your community stay healthy.

The flu vaccine is given free on the NHS to people who:

  • Are 65 and over (including those who'll be 65 by 31 March).
  • Have certain health conditions.
  • Are pregnant.
  • Are in long-stay residential care.
  • Receive a carer's allowance, or are the main carer for an older or disabled person who may be at risk if you get sick.
  • Live with someone who is more likely to get infections (such as someone who has HIV, has had a transplant or is having certain treatments for cancer, lupus or rheumatoid arthritis).
  • Are frontline health or social care workers.
